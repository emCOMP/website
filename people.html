Principal Investigators

HCDE Kate Starbird

Kate combines computational methods and qualitiative methods to understand social phenomenon at scale. One major focus of her work examines the use of social media during crisis events, specifically looking at how the converging audience (aka, the "crowd") can contribute—and is already contributing—to crisis response efforts.

iSchool Emma Spiro

Emma studies online communication and information-related behaviors in the context of emergencies and disaster events. Her work also explores the structure and dynamics of interpersonal and organizational networks in both online and offline environments.

iSchool Robert Mason

Robert's current research interests focus on the philosophy and ethics of technology management and the cultural aspects of knowledge management. His recent research examines the impact of social media on knowledge work and how organizations provide post-implementation support for enterprise systems. He is a founding member of the Social Media Lab @ UW (somelab.net) and is working with a team of students on information flows related to the Occupy movement.

Graduate Students

HCDE Ahmer Arif

Ahmer is interested in studying how sharing and consuming information around crisis events on social media affects people on an emotional level. Ahmer really digs spending time with his wife, playing board-games and being a total foodie.

Comm Elodie Fichet

Elodie's research focuses on Crisis Communication and how the use of new technologies has affected crisis response from both the professional and public realms. She often takes a qualitative perspective on the lab's research streams. She has worked - and continues to work - on self-corrective behaviors on Twitter (in response to rumor propagation) as well as the impact of 'official accounts' on rumoring during crisis situations.

CSE A. Conrad Nied

Conrad is most excited about bringing more tools for interacting with the crisis data and using natural language processing to understands patterns in the data. Conrad is generally interested in personalized user feedback in CMC. Out of school, he likes to bake cookies, dance, and play with his cat Simba.

HCDE John Robinson

John is interested in design considerations for emergency response technologies. He comes to his PhD studies with over 15 years of software development experience including the first internet-streaming consumer-electronics devices. John spends a lot of time thinking about information visualization, emergent intelligences, decision making, and virtual collaborations. In his spare time, he volunteers with various youth and outreach programs. He also enjoys films, games, and music.

iSchool Li Zeng

Li is interested in implementing data analytics to understand rumoring transmission behaviors and developing machine learning strategies to automatically identify rumors on social media. She is currently working on designing machine learning experiments to identify rumor-affirming tweets and rumor-denying tweets for the project.
